# Demo video script — 60–90s

The universal reusable asset (idea #20). Goal: show a real query → cited
answer → **the cite tracing back to retrieved source text**. That trace-back
moment is the whole pitch — linger on it.

Record at 1080p, no audio needed (captions instead, so it autoplays silently
on LinkedIn/X). Keep the case file generic — no PII on screen.

---

## Beat sheet

**0:00–0:08 — The hook (cold open on the problem)**
> On-screen text: *"In 2023 a lawyer was sanctioned for citing 6 cases that
> didn't exist. An AI made them up."*
Cut to: the lawagent chat box, empty.

**0:08–0:20 — Ask a real question**
Type a real query, e.g.:
> *"What factors does Connecticut weigh when awarding alimony?"*
Show the agent thinking (retrieve → reason → cite). Caption: *"It only
answers from documents it actually retrieved."*

**0:20–0:45 — The grounded answer**
Answer renders with inline cites in standard CT form:
> *Conn. Gen. Stat. § 46b-82(a)* … *Smith v. Smith, 333 Conn. 1 (2019)* …
Caption: *"Every claim carries a citation."*

**0:45–1:05 — The trace-back (the money shot)**
Click a citation → side panel opens showing the **exact retrieved chunk** the
claim came from, statute text highlighted.
Caption: *"Click any cite — see the source text it's grounded in. No
hallucinated cases. Ever."*

**1:05–1:20 — Who it's for + close**
> On-screen text: *"Built for people representing themselves. Not legal
> advice — checkable legal research."*
End card: project name + URL + "built solo · LangGraph · pgvector · AWS".

---

## Caption file
Keep captions in a `.srt` next to the recording so the same cut reposts to
LinkedIn, X, and the landing page.

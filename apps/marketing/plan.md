# GTM plan — organic, legal/startup community

A plan to generate, prioritize, and execute advertising ideas. Audience and
constraints are in [README](README.md).

## Phase 0 — Lock the positioning
The hook for this audience:

> **"Grounded, checkable citations for pro se litigants — the
> anti-hallucination legal assistant."**

Why it lands: *Mata v. Avianca* (ChatGPT fake-citation sanctions) made this
community anxious about hallucinated cites. lawagent's architecture
(every cite traces back to a retrieved chunk) is a direct answer.

Decisions: (1) one-sentence pitch; (2) frame as product / open-source /
case study — that choice changes which channels fit.

## Phase 1 — Map the ideation space
Brainstorm inside six fixed channel buckets (~5 ideas each):
A2J ecosystem · legaltech press/blogs · builder/startup · technical
content/SEO · social/network · direct outreach. See [ideas.md](ideas.md).

## Phase 2 — Score & pick
Rate each idea 1–5 on four axes; take the top 3–5:
- **Reach** into the legal/startup audience
- **Effort** (inverted — organic = your time is the budget)
- **Proof** (credibility / demo value generated)
- **Compound** (keeps paying off vs one-shot)

## Phase 3 — Build the proof assets (reusable middle)
Most channels consume the same 3 assets — build once:
1. 60–90s **demo video** (real query → cited answer → cite traces back).
2. One **flagship technical post**.
3. A clean **public landing page** (problem, demo, "not legal advice").

## Phase 4 — 4-week organic cadence
- **Wk 1:** ship demo + landing.
- **Wk 2:** publish flagship post → Show HN / r/legaltech.
- **Wk 3:** A2J outreach + LinkedIn/X thread.
- **Wk 4:** pitch one legaltech blog; measure; double down on what moved.

## Phase 5 — Measure
Pick 2–3 signals before starting: demo views, inbound DMs/emails from the
target audience, GitHub stars (if OSS), waitlist signups. Judge organic by
*quality* of inbound (e.g. a clinic director replies) over raw traffic.

## Dual-use note
This audience overlaps with the **job search** — a technical build-in-public
post lands with legaltech builders *and* hiring managers. The flagship post
and LinkedIn asset do double duty.
